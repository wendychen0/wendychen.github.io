About Me

As part of my Computer Science Game Design capstone, I collaborated with a group of students to develop a 2D platformer game in Unity. We have been working on this project for two quarters and are currently expanding the game's worlds in preparation for a Steam release in the coming months. The game, titled Three Bulky Bears, features a unique mechanic where players can switch between three characters with different abilities, such as varying speeds and jump heights, to navigate and overcome environmental obstacles in each level.

This summer, leveraging my prior experience as an intern, I took on a mentorship role in the CITRIS Workforce Innovation Program. I guide a cohort of six students interning at various labs and companies, supporting them in both one-on-one and group settings. I support them with their program workshop assignments, which focus on career planning, design thinking, and presenting research. I also lead workshops on goal and career planning, offering guidance and encouragement throughout the process.

During college, I held leadership positions as President of the Association for Computing Machinery's Council on Women (ACM-W) and as Fundraising Chair and Treasurer for the Society of Women Engineers (SWE) at UCSC. In these roles, I organized and led workshops on resume writing, web development, and project management, supporting the growth of women in engineering and computing. I led the initiative in securing scholarships for members to attend the Grace Hopper Conference, fostering a supportive community for women in STEM. These experiences allowed me to work closely with diverse teams, collaborating effectively to achieve our shared goals.

In addition, I managed a six-figure custom apparel business as a Campus Manager for Fresh Prints. I handled client relations, guided the design process, and ensured that products met customer expectations, showcasing my continuous learner attitude by adapting to new challenges and improving business strategies.

Now, I am seeking full-time software roles where I can apply my skills and experiences to impactful real-world projects. I continue to develop Three Bulky Bears, mentor interns, and maintain a balanced lifestyle by running and spending time with family and friends. My passion for learning and collaboration drives me to create meaningful products that people use and enjoy daily.
