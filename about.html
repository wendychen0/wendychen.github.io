As part of my Computer Science Game Design capstone, I started developing a 2D platformer in Unity with a group of other students. We've been in development for 2 quarters and are currently working to create more worlds and prepare for a release on Steam in the coming months. The game is called Three Bulky Bears, where you switch between 3 characters at the press of a button. You change how fast you move, how high you jump, and interact with the environment to overcome obstacles and beat each level.

This summer I started as a mentor for the CITRIS Workforce Innovation Program having interned with them in the past. I mentor a cohort of 6 students who intern at various labs and companies. I proactively engage with my mentees in both 1 on 1 and group meetings and support them with their program workshop assignments, which focus on career planning, design thinking, and presenting research. I also lead workshops on goal and career planning, offering guidance and encouragement throughout the process.

In college, I was the President of the Association for Computing Machinery's Council on Women (ACM-W) for 2 years and Fundraising Chair and Treasurer for the Society of Women Engineers (SWE) at UCSC. Both organizations provide workshops and resources to support and help women succeed in the field of engineering and computing. I lead weekly board meetings to plan and organize club events and workshops and present to members on topics like writing a resume, web development fundamentals and mini projects. I also presented to various funding bodies on campus through ACM-W to provide scholarships for members to attend the Grace Hopper Conference. Through these roles, I aimed to foster a community for like-minded women and hoped to bring more women into STEM careers.

I worked as a Campus Manager for Fresh Prints, a custom apparel company for the majority of my time in college. I operated a six-figure custom apparel business, serving organizations both on and off campus. I guided hundreds of clients through the design process, recommended products, and negotiated pricing to ensure customer satisfaction. Additionally, I communicated with customers and internal teams to align with clients' visions and meet deadlines.

Now, I'm looking for full time roles in the software field, while continuing to develop Three Bulky Bears, mentoring interns, going on runs, and finding time for family and friends! I hope to apply my skills and experiences to work on impactful real world projects.
