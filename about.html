As a Campus Manager for Fresh Prints, a custom apparel company and have been pitching and working with over 30 organizations on and off campus. I help recommend products and work with customers through the design/proof process. For each client, I utilize my work ethic and great attention to detail to ensure that every order thoroughly met the customers’ visions and deadlines.

Through my leadership positions in various clubs in high school and college, I have become a tactful communicator with experience interacting and working with people from diverse backgrounds. In highschool, I was the Treasurer and Vice President of Bake for Charity, a club started by my friends and I. We raised raised and donated money to various charities and organizations such as the Malala Fund and the Bay Area Rescue Mission through bi-weekly bake sales. I also became the co-head for lighting my senior year, but helped program and run the lights for school dances and plays for 3 years. In my senior year, I was a facilitator for a local Girls Who Code club, where I helped teach about 25 elementary/middle school girls to implement game projects coded with Scratch, Micro Bits, and HTML on a weekly basis.

I'm currently the President of The Association for Computing Machinery's Council on Women (ACM-W) and Fundraising Chair for The Society of Women Engineers (SWE) at UCSC. Both organizations provide workshops and resources to support and help women succeed in the field of engineering and computing. I participate in weekly board meetings to plan and organize club events and workshops and present to members on topics like writing a resume and HTML basics. Through these positions I hope to bring more women into stem careers.

Now, I'm trying my best to manage my undergraduate coursework, while being involved in clubs, working as a researcher for the ASSIST Lab, and trying to meet new people and enjoy my time in college!
